It also explains how participants were seated at tables, the principles governing session organization, and the role and duties of moderators.

Organization principles

The purpose of the Forum is to develop short-, medium-, and long-term strategies that will guide WAAS's future activities. In other words, if one may put it this way, the Forum's 'output' should be several strategic plans.

Within the events planned for 10 September, a series of morning and post-lunch sessions has been scheduled with the participation of international guests:

In the morning sessions, groups are formed as MIXED groups — that is, participants are divided into groups regardless of their field of science, specialty, or country of residence.

  • In the post-lunch sessions, participants work in groups aligned with their fields of science (these may be called Discipline-Specific Groups).
  • Organizing groups in these two ways will contribute to participant networking. For example, participants who joined MIXED groups may later find themselves in one of the Discipline-Specific Groups. This, in turn, will enable participants to connect with more colleagues and broaden their networks.
  • Before discussions begin, moderators must introduce participants to the audience, providing very brief (10-second) information on each person's name, field of science, workplace, and scientific achievements.
  • Sessions are conducted using the brainstorming method.
  • Each table will display a sign indicating its number. Name plates showing each participant's name and country of representation will be placed on the tables.
  • During Discipline-Specific group discussions, additional signs indicating the relevant fields of science will also be placed on the tables.
  • Sessions will be moderated by members of the WAAS Executive Board and other designated (selected) participants. Moderators will guide discussions and overall management in both MIXED and Discipline-Specific Groups. To record ideas and proposals voiced by participants, one scribe will be selected at each table. While moderators remain responsible for their groups as a whole, scribes will be responsible for capturing table discussions on paper. The number of moderators will remain the same, while the number of scribes will equal the number of tables. At the end of the sessions, moderators will collect the scribes' notes, synthesize them, and present them to the audience on behalf of their groups.

MIXED groups — discussion topics

MIXED groups

  • They discuss the short-, medium-, and long-term strategies shown as examples in the "STRATEGIC PLANNING DIRECTIONS" section and select those most suitable for implementation;
  • Taking into account issues raised in the presentations of state officials and university leaders;
  • They produce a draft containing provisions reflecting the group's preferred strategic directions and explaining why they are appropriate.

STRATEGIC PLANNING DIRECTIONS

As Azerbaijani intellectuals living in different countries around the world, our aim in organizing and conducting the forum is to contribute to Azerbaijan through the development and implementation of short-, medium-, and long-term strategic plans in science and technology, international law and relations, energy, education, healthcare, social sciences, and culture.

The following directions may be considered for the development of strategic plans:

Short-term strategies (1-2 years):

  • Data Collection and Analysis Across Relevant Fields
  • Analysing the current situation and identifying key challenges
  • Establishing cooperation with key stakeholders (government, universities, non-governmental organizations, etc.)
  • Networking and Communication
  • Building relationships with intellectuals and organizations sharing common interests
  • Organizing information exchange through online platforms and social media
  • Pilot Projects
  • Designing and implementing small-scale pilot projects
  • Gaining experience for large-scale projects by evaluating outcomes

Medium-term strategies (3-5 years)

  • Development Programmes
  • Organizing training programmes and seminars
  • Organizing exchange of experience between local and international experts
  • Research and Innovation
  • Establishing funds for scientific research or building cooperation with existing funds
  • Creating platforms for the application of innovative technologies
  • Public Education and Awareness
  • Organizing awareness campaigns in education and healthcare
  • Implementing projects in social sciences and culture through cooperation with local communities

Long-term strategies (5+ years)

  • Sustainable Development
  • Implementation of sustainable and long-term projects
  • Expanding cooperation at regional and global levels
  • National Strategy and Institutional Development
  • Participating in the preparation and implementation of nationwide strategic development plans
  • Establishing new research centres and institutes
  • Resource Mobilization and Management
  • Creating mechanisms for the sustained mobilization and management of resources
  • Preparing long-term investment programmes, particularly in energy and education
  • Evaluation of Results
  • Regularly evaluating outcomes of completed work and preparing reports
  • Reviewing and improving strategies based on achieved results

Discipline-Specific Groups — discussion topics

Discipline-Specific Groups

  • The priority strategic directions identified by the MIXED groups;
  • Issues raised in the presentations of state officials and university leaders; and
  • By reviewing topics listed in the "RECOMMENDED DISCUSSION TOPICS BY FIELD OF SCIENCE" section, they prepare and produce WAAS's short-, medium-, and long-term strategic plans.

RECOMMENDED DISCUSSION TOPICS BY FIELD OF SCIENCE

Literature, Social Sciences, Theology

  • Digital Publishing and Literary Platforms
    • Development of e-books, textbooks, and digital publishing platforms
    • Creating digital discussion forums and literary platforms
  • Innovation Platforms
    • Organizing collaboration with global research networks in social sciences
    • Creating platforms for the application of innovative technologies in social sectors
  • Strengthening Social Infrastructure
    • Improving social services and expanding their accessibility for the population
    • Applying digital technologies to ensure citizens' active participation in social processes
  • Cognitive Research and Data Science
    • Studying human behaviour, decision-making, and their interdependence
    • Use of Big (Huge) Data and analytical methods in social sciences
  • Digital Theology and Religious Studies
    • Digitizing religious data and creating online discussion platforms
    • Expanding research on the role and impact of religion in contemporary society

Economics and Management

  • Data Analytics and Digital Governance:
    • Using Big (Huge) Data for economic forecasting and market analysis
    • Ensuring the use of digital technologies in state-building and governance
  • Digital Economy
    • Developing and implementing digital payment systems, blockchain technologies, and fintech solutions
    • Ensuring cybersecurity in finance and management
  • Innovation and Technology
    • Ensuring the use of technologies such as artificial intelligence, Big (Huge) Data, and quantum computing in economics and management
    • Applying digital and smart technologies in the management of regions and cities
  • Resource Management
    • Developing strategies for the efficient management and protection of water and other limited resources
    • Developing and implementing long-term green industrial investment programmes in the energy sector

Humanities, Education, Arts, History

  • Digitalization of Cultural Heritage
    • Implementing digital projects for the preservation, promotion, and dissemination of cultural heritage
    • Applying Big (Huge) Data analysis for social research and surveys
  • Digital Culture Centres
    • By establishing digital culture centres, promoting our national culture at local and global levels and instilling our national and spiritual values
    • Developing digital research methods in the humanities
  • Global Cultural Relations, Digital Learning Resources
    • Creating digital platforms to promote Azerbaijani culture and history globally
    • Creation of online education platforms.
    • Advancing the teaching of STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Matematics) subjects to leading international standards
  • Digital Teaching Programmes
    • Preparing textbooks and instructional materials on digital technologies
    • Enriching curricula with modern technologies
    • Organizing training for teachers on digital teaching technologies
  • Global Education Partnerships
    • Establishing cooperation with leading international universities and research centres
    • Ensuring the advancement of Azerbaijani universities in global ranking systems
    • Establishing cooperation with foreign universities and research centres; increasing the number of Azerbaijani students studying and conducting research there, and ensuring their prospects in Azerbaijan
  • Sustainable Education Infrastructure
    • Developing digital and physical educational infrastructures
    • Creating sustainable innovation programmes in education
  • Archaeological Research and Digital History
    • Supporting new archaeological research and excavations
    • Digitization and archiving of historical records

Law

  • Academic Partnerships:
    • Establishing scholarship programmes to raise specialized education programmes to advanced global standards
    • Organizing seminars and conferences by inviting local and international experts on important topics
  • Research Centres and Institutes
    • Establishing specialized research centres and institutes and conducting research in those centres aligned with Azerbaijan's interests
    • Participating in joint research projects on global issues and ensuring an increase in the number of scientific articles published in this field
  • Legislative and Policy Recommendations
    • Preparing recommendations to strengthen the country's legislative framework in the field of law and submitting them to state authorities
    • Contributing to the drafting of legislative acts in the field of law
  • Sustainable Development and Adaptation
    • Continuously updating and developing strategic plans in the field of law in line with changing global conditions
    • In line with sustainable development principles, ensuring the implementation of long-term strategies and supporting new innovative approaches in this field
  • Cybersecurity and Digital Law
    • Developing robust cybersecurity laws to ensure national security
    • Improving the legal framework for digital property and information security

Medicine and Related Fields

  • Telemedicine Services
    • Organizing telemedicine services through digital platforms
    • Establishing electronic medical databases
  • Medical Information Systems
    • Expanding electronic health records and data exchange systems
    • Applying artificial intelligence technologies in diagnostic and treatment processes
  • Sustainable Medical Infrastructure
    • Continuously improving digital healthcare infrastructures
    • Ensuring the participation of our scholars in international health research
  • Medical Research and Technologies
    • Supporting bioinformatics and genetic research
    • Genetic Analysis: Development of genome research and personalized treatment programmes
    • Regenerative Medicine: Supporting projects in cell and tissue engineering
    • Biomedical Engineering: Development and implementation of health technologies

Natural Sciences, Engineering and Related Fields

  • Technology Transfer
    • Applying technologies in Azerbaijan by drawing on international best practices
    • Creating support programmes for startups
  • Innovation Centres
    • Establishing research and innovation centres (R&D-oriented)
    • Developing funds and grant programmes for innovative project development
  • Development of Research Infrastructure
    • Establishing research laboratories in artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, and other advanced technologies
    • Preparing a national strategy for sustainable research, green industry, and innovation
    • Participation in international research programmes and strengthening cooperation
  • Digital Transformation, Monitoring, and Management
    • Implementing digital transformation strategies
    • Creating an ecosystem for the development of the digital economy (an environment that supports the broad dissemination and use of digital technologies and innovations)
    • Building systems for monitoring and managing energy production and consumption through Internet of Things (IoT) and Big (Huge) Data technologies
    • Implementing small pilot projects in renewable energy sources
  • Smart Grids
    • Applying smart-grid technologies in energy distribution systems
    • Creating digital management systems to improve energy efficiency
  • Green Energy
    • Organizing promotion and advocacy activities to expand the use of renewable energy sources
    • Developing efficient energy-use systems within smart city projects
  • Advancing Climate Research and Astrobiology
    • Expanding research on climate change and sustainable development
    • Participation in space research and international cooperation
  • Robotics and Artificial Intelligence
    • Using robots and artificial intelligence in production processes
    • Applying digital technologies to maintain control over the quality of water, air, and food products
    • Development and implementation of health technologies
