Ceyran MAHMUDOVA

Rector of Azerbaijan State University of Culture and Arts; Honored Art Worker; Doctor of Sciences in Art Studies; Professor

In 2001, at the initiative of our National Leader Heydar Aliyev, the First Congress of World Azerbaijanis was held. In subsequent years, these congresses continued by the orders and with the support of President Ilham Aliyev. In 2022, the Fifth Congress of World Azerbaijanis was already held. The First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad, held in Baku, Khankendi and Shusha in September 2024, should be regarded as a very important event in terms of bringing world Azerbaijanis together in a new format.

The message of the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan, Mr. Ilham Aliyev, to the forum participants emphasized the great importance of this event and added a special spirit to its proceedings.

I had the honor of taking part in the First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad, held in Baku in September 2024, and I returned from the event with very pleasant impressions and interesting ideas. Most importantly, our compatriots living far from the Motherland, on different continents of the world, are not only bearers of our national heritage and cultural values, but also their promoters.

At the forum, we became acquainted with the activities and achievements of our compatriots in various fields of science. It is very gratifying that our scientists have been able to take a worthy place in the international scientific arena and gain recognition. The scientific and theoretical reports discussed, as well as the effective proposals made for the more active integration of modern Azerbaijani science and education into the international scientific and educational space, were highly valuable.

The forum gave me the opportunity to make unforgettable acquaintances with our scientists living in different countries, and at the same time to meet colleagues whom I had known for many years but had not seen for a long time.

I would like to express special gratitude to Masud Efendiyev, Chair of WAAS, a mathematician recognized in the international scientific community, the first Azerbaijani laureate of the Humboldt Prize, a scientist whose name has entered history, and a patriotic and modest personality, for his great efforts in bringing together Azerbaijani scientists living in different countries and ensuring that all events within the forum were held at a high level.

There is an aphorism: 'Beauty will save the world.' Relating this saying to the leitmotif of peace and tranquility in the speeches at the forum, I can say that education, science and culture can save our troubled world today.

Until we meet at the next forum!

Adalat MURADOV

Rector of Azerbaijan State University of Economics, Professor

Dear colleagues, distinguished scientists, honored guests!

First of all, on behalf of UNEC, I greet each of you on the occasion of this prestigious forum of the World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists. The gathering of Azerbaijani scientists working in various fields of science in different corners of the world is a celebration of our solidarity, intellectual potential and national identity as a nation. This forum is not only an academic discussion platform; it is also an important stage at which the foundations of scientific and technological strategies for the future of Azerbaijan are being laid.

By coming together here today, we are not only sharing our knowledge, but also jointly designing the future of our science. The participation in this forum of Azerbaijani scientists working at prestigious scientific centers around the world proves that Azerbaijan's intellectual diaspora has already become part of the international system.

I believe that this association will not be limited to an informative purpose; it will become a format of cooperation that produces practical results. By building a strong worldwide network of Azerbaijani scientists, we can form national centers of strength in the field of science. This is an important component of both our diaspora policy and our academic diplomacy.

In order for the forum to be oriented toward concrete results, I would like to bring the following proposals to your attention:

1. An interactive portal should be created that brings together the database, fields of activity, scientific works and areas of interest of Azerbaijani scientists worldwide. This would facilitate cooperation and also serve the recognition of Azerbaijani science on the global map;

2. A mechanism of financial support should be formed for joint research to be conducted with the participation of Azerbaijani scientists around the world;

3. I propose that, as an outcome of the forum, a program entitled 'World Azerbaijani Young Scientists Mentorship Initiative' be launched. Within this program, experienced scientists will guide young Azerbaijani researchers and support their academic and professional development;

4. If our medium- and long-term goal is to achieve scientific success at the level of the Nobel Prize, institutional programs should be prepared for systematic work in this direction. Both foreign and local academic resources should be mobilized here.

This forum is a moment when our national identity is united with scientific potential and when the idea of Azerbaijanism speaks in the language of science. May this gathering become a symbol: a symbol of Azerbaijan being known in the world not only for oil and gas, but also for science, innovation and artificial intelligence. As UNEC, we are determined to deepen this cooperation further.

May this forum be not the beginning, but the continuation and rise of the scientific solidarity of world Azerbaijanis!

Thank you.

Elbrus ISAYEV

Rector of Nakhchivan State University; PhD in History; Associate Professor

Science and education are the foundation of every nation's development and the most reliable guide on the road to the future. Scientists, who travel this road, are people who expand the boundaries of society's thinking, create scientific innovations and change the world. In Azerbaijan, too, science and education have always been given great importance, and many outstanding scientists have been raised.

Throughout history, the Azerbaijani people have treated science and enlightenment with deep respect and have been proud of their renowned scholars. Today, our scientists living in different countries of the world not only have their say in the international scientific environment, but also worthily represent our national identity and intellectual potential. In recent years, Azerbaijani scientists working outside our country in various scientific and research centers have honorably represented both Azerbaijan and the Turkic world through their scientific achievements.

The World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists (WAAS) plays an important role in coordinating the activities of scientists living abroad, organizing the exchange of experience and bringing them together. This association was created to strengthen the scientific, cultural and spiritual ties of Azerbaijani scientists with one another and with the Motherland, and it has undertaken a number of global initiatives. Thanks to the initiatives of WAAS, the efforts of our scientists working in all parts of the world are being united in a more systematic and effective way.

The holding of the First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad in Baku on 9 September 2024, with the support of the State Committee on Work with Diaspora and the Ministry of Science and Education and organized by the World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists, was of great importance in bringing together our scientists living in different countries, introducing them to local scientists, exchanging knowledge and experience, and contributing to the networking of Azerbaijani scientists worldwide. The congratulatory letter sent by the head of state, President Ilham Aliyev, was an indication of the significance of this forum and the attention paid to it.

The organization of the Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad and the gathering in Baku of our scientists working in different parts of the world remained in memory as an important event. The forum brought together more than 80 Azerbaijani scientists living abroad from 23 countries and was important for further increasing the prestige of Azerbaijani science and education and for applying global innovations in science and education.

In this regard, it was commendable that Professor Masud Efendiyev of the Technical University of Munich, Germany, the first Azerbaijani scientist to receive the Humboldt Prize, Honored Scientist, Doctor of Physical and Mathematical Sciences and a world-renowned scholar, stated at the event that the main purpose of organizing the forum was to contribute to the development of Azerbaijani science, to build a bridge between experienced and young scientists, and to unite efforts for the further strengthening of Azerbaijan's international reputation and the high-level development of our science.

The forum once again showed that considerable positive work has been done in the field of science and education in Azerbaijan in recent years, and that the participation of Azerbaijani scientists living abroad in raising the level of our science and training personnel in line with the requirements of the new era is one of today's key demands.

It was one of the most pleasant feelings for us when the world-renowned Azerbaijani scientist Masud Efendiyev paid special attention to the visions of Nakhchivan State University, said that they were more attractive and interesting to him, expressed a personal wish to come to Nakhchivan and see the university with his own eyes, and stated what work we should do to implement mutual cooperation between WAAS and Nakhchivan State University.

In one of his interviews, Masud muallim noted: 'This ancient land has given our country very valuable scientists. One of the main reasons for my visit to Nakhchivan was precisely this. Those scientists have indeed done great work in matters of vital importance for Azerbaijan. It was very important for me to see with my own eyes the place where such valuable personalities were born. I have already been in Nakhchivan for two days. We exchanged views on the work planned by the Board of the Forum of World Azerbaijani Scientists. What pleased me most was that, upon my arrival at Nakhchivan State University, the first place they took me to was the university library. I think this is a certain symbol. It was an approach characteristic of leading universities... While in Germany, I had told Elbrus muallim that I wanted to communicate not only with students but also with schoolchildren. They are our future. What points should be touched upon for their future education? All this interested me and I wanted to learn it. In my opinion, direct communication is both right and meaningful. I wanted to obtain all information not from teachers, but directly from the pupils. I positively assess the preservation of Azerbaijan's ancient traditions and the exhibitions there. Overall, I am very pleased to be here.'

On 17-18 December 2024, Masud Efendiyev visited Nakhchivan State University. During the meetings held on his first visit to Nakhchivan, the professor spoke about the creation and goals of the World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists and noted that the association serves to strengthen the position of Azerbaijani scientists in the world scientific community and to expand cooperation among them.

During the visit, the Chair of WAAS, the well-known scientist Masud Efendiyev, visited the university's Scientific Library and International School, Nakhchivan Girls' Lyceum, the House Museum of Academician Y. Mammadaliyev in Ordubad and a number of other historical sites. At the meeting at the International School of Nakhchivan State University, the professor spoke to pupils about his scientific and international experience and encouraged them to show greater interest in science and education. He noted that every pupil has great potential and that patience and constant work are needed to reveal it. Speaking about his work at the Helmholtz Research Center in Munich and his participation in various international projects, the professor said that cooperation on international platforms creates great opportunities for students to increase their knowledge and skills.

One of the important events during the visit was the signing of a cooperation agreement between Nakhchivan State University and the World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists. For many years, thanks to its foreign relations, Nakhchivan State University has attracted dozens of highly qualified professors every year to scientific and pedagogical work at the university. However, the fact that professors and highly qualified specialists coming from abroad are Azerbaijanis will always create positive impressions of the presence and prestige of Azerbaijani science in the world. Within the framework of the signed cooperation agreement, WAAS and Nakhchivan State University defined as their main goals and priorities: involving Azerbaijani scientists living abroad in teaching at NSU; preparing student exchange programs that provide double-degree opportunities in accordance with the legislation of the countries where the parties are located; conducting joint scientific research with Azerbaijani scientists working at various higher education institutions worldwide; creating a section entitled 'Azerbaijani scientists living abroad' on NSU's official website and placing information about them in Azerbaijani and English; regularly enriching the university library's fund 'Scientific works of Azerbaijani scientists living abroad' with their works; creating in the media center an archive of video lectures by Azerbaijani scientists living abroad; ensuring that WAAS regularly organizes trainings and seminars at NSU by member scientists; using the capacities of WAAS member scientists in master's and doctoral training; exchanging academic research data and technical materials; cooperating in cultural activities; organizing joint academic and scientific events such as conferences, seminars, symposia and congresses; and promoting cooperation between NSU and higher education institutions in the countries where WAAS member scientists live.

Within the framework of the cooperation agreement with WAAS, visits to Nakhchivan by doctors living abroad who are members of the association have been organized twice. With their participation, master classes, scientific seminars and conferences were held at the university's Faculty of Medicine.

In conclusion, such significant initiatives in the path of science and education - especially the cooperation established between WAAS and Nakhchivan State University - are an important step in promoting our national scientific potential internationally and educating future generations. Sharing in the homeland the knowledge and experience acquired by Azerbaijani scientists in various scientific centers of the world, building intellectual bridges and directing the younger generation toward science provide strong impetus to our country's sustainable development. These forms of cooperation are not limited to expanding scientific ties; they also strengthen our national identity, cultural heritage and attachment to the homeland. Our united efforts in the path of science are among the most valuable steps taken for a brighter, more knowledgeable and more successful Azerbaijan of the future.

Elchin BABAYEV

Rector of Baku State University; PhD in Physics and Mathematics; Associate Professor

Strengthening ties with Azerbaijani scientists currently living abroad and making significant contributions to the development of world science is of great importance. On the one hand, it enables the creation of platforms for science, education and exchange of experience and the further development of diaspora organizations. On the other hand, by involving them in close and effective cooperation with our country's scientific and educational institutions, it helps integrate advanced international experience and scientific-technological innovations into our science and education environment, increase the scientific productivity of universities and research institutes, and strengthen their position in the global scientific space.

WAAS, including our scientists living abroad, can create additional functional and sustainable opportunities for cooperation in linking universities and research institutes in our country with the international academic environment in the following areas:

organization and implementation of joint activities to determine research priorities, taking into account the country's socio-economic needs, regional and global scientific priorities, as well as the human resources, infrastructure and technical capacities of universities and research institutes;

creation in the country of professional and qualified human resources that meet the demands of the modern global labor market;

establishment of an integrated modern scientific and technological ecosystem that includes education-research-industry links among universities and research institutes; support for institutional initiatives in this direction; and creation of opportunities for the joint and effective use of research infrastructure;

development, with the support of the state, industry and private sector, of targeted scholarship mechanisms for professional development and internship programs in leading foreign scientific and educational centers in priority areas;

creation of joint research laboratories or centers in priority scientific fields and organization and implementation of result-oriented research activities based on common programs and protocols;

alignment of the relevant policy with international standards and provision of methodological support for the indexing process so that scientific journals in Azerbaijan can be included in international databases such as Scopus, Web of Science and others;

conclusion of scientific cooperation agreements with foreign universities and research centers, including institutions where our diaspora scientists work; within these agreements, planning and financing scientific and technical projects based on mutual interests and ensuring that scientific results are reflected in joint publications and patents.

At the same time, the following additional mechanisms may also be effective in strengthening cooperation with diaspora scientists:

creation of a unified platform or virtual networks for diaspora scientists to establish contacts with universities and scientific institutions in our country; provision of institutional and financial support from state structures and industrial/business partners for the sustainable operation of the platform;

inviting diaspora scientists to deliver papers at various scientific events organized by universities and research institutes, creating conditions for exchange of experience among scientists, and holding seminars, master classes and meetings devoted to scientific innovations, trends and new technologies with their participation;

development of targeted grant programs that promote the integration of diaspora scientists into scientific activities in Azerbaijan and formation of sustainable financing mechanisms;

formation of a legal and institutional basis for foreign scientists to conduct temporary teaching and research activities at Azerbaijani universities with guest or visiting professor status;

development of special programs for our scientists working abroad to mentor local researchers; offering special contracts and incentive packages to our diaspora scientists for integration into Azerbaijan's research and education system;

creation of support mechanisms for our diaspora scientists to apply for and obtain international grants for joint projects;

expansion of opportunities for local scientists to access foreign grant programs and optimization of the application process.

Baku State University cooperates with Azerbaijani scientists working abroad in various formats. Within this cooperation, these scientists are invited as experts to international scientific events, and open lectures and thematic seminars are held with their participation. They are also involved as scientific advisers in the joint supervision of doctoral students. In addition, they make direct scientific and organizational contributions to the preparation and implementation of international scientific projects and to the dissemination of scientific results.

Hamlet ISAKHANLI

Rector of Khazar University, Professor

The Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad, held in the capital city of Baku, is one of the most remarkable events for Azerbaijani science and higher education. In today's world, science and technology, rather than the economy alone, come to the forefront in the competition among countries, because the modern economy is a product of science and technology. It is difficult for a country without advanced science and technology to have a sustainable and competitive economy. The development of science takes place in higher education institutions and universities. During the socialist period, the Academy of Sciences was also an important center of science. Today, however, it either changes completely and becomes a club of scientists without research institutes, as in the Baltic countries and Georgia, or it experiences crisis, gradually changing its form and essence, becoming smaller and weaker, as in Azerbaijan.

What should we do, and where are we going? The forum organized by WAAS with the support of the State Committee on Work with Diaspora and the Ministry of Science and Education will probably discuss these vital questions and try to find reasonable answers and recommendations.

What kind of higher education institution do we want? The natural answer that comes to mind is: a strong one. But what does a strong higher education institution mean? 'Strong' is not a precise expression. 'High-quality' is somewhat more understandable. Although quality is not unambiguous, it can be measured. For quality, a higher education institution must have good people and material resources. We need to be more precise:

1) A higher education institution must have good students - talented and hardworking people who come to learn.

2) It must have good teachers - talented scientists who teach.

3) What will the teacher teach the student, and how? The higher education institution must have a good educational model, curricula and programs aligned with the problems of the modern world.

4) It must have good conditions and an attractive environment. A sufficient material base, information resources, serious laboratories and a healthy and appealing environment are essential elements defining a higher education institution.

Which of these measures of quality is the most important? The universal answer is: all of them. If pressed, I would say that the talented scientist is decisive. Good and talented students will come to a higher education institution where a recognized scientist works; just as a patient chooses a doctor, a student chooses a good teacher and strives to learn from him or her. If there is good management and recognized scientists in a higher education institution, an educational model will be chosen by benefiting from world experience. Finally, as these matters improve, the conditions and environment at the institution will also gradually improve and strengthen.

When founding the University of Berlin, now Humboldt University, in 1809-1810, Wilhelm von Humboldt expressed the main parameters of a good university as follows: 'A strong scientist is invited, conditions are created for him, while teaching students he includes them in his creative environment, the student can choose some teachers and courses, and finally, although the state finances the university, it does not interfere in its affairs.'

Thus, it is impossible to build a high-quality higher education institution without good scientists and specialists. It is not enough to rely only on scientists working in Azerbaijani universities. In a period when science has branched out so widely and many new fields have emerged, work cannot progress unless there are strong scientists in every field. One of the tasks of WAAS is to fill these sparse ranks of scientists. It is necessary to work with Azerbaijani universities that are willing to attract not only Azerbaijani scientists working abroad, which is still not enough, but also leading scientists working at foreign universities in their respective fields. Another important issue is to help establish cooperation with good universities around the world, including the organization of double-degree programs.

As Khazar University, we have tried to follow the principles of quality higher education that I have mentioned. With great pleasure, I express our desire to cooperate closely with WAAS and to contribute together, even if modestly, to the development of our homeland and our people.

Thank you.

Acting Rector of Azerbaijan State Oil and Industry University at the time of the Forum; Associate Professor

First of all, as a member of the diaspora who lived abroad for a long time, I would like to note that the holding for the first time in Azerbaijan of the Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad in Baku, Khankendi and Shusha became an event of great historical importance. The event brought together more than 80 scientists from 23 countries and nearly 200 scientific figures in total. implementation of exchange programs for ASOIU students, teachers and doctoral students at educational institutions where WAAS member scientists work;

organization of training and professional development programs for both sides from the diaspora and local universities;

creation of joint technology and innovation centers, laboratories and technoparks to support startups and technological projects;

On 9-11 September 2024, an unforgettable and strategically important event took place in the history of Azerbaijan - the First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad was held. Covering Baku, Khankendi and Shusha, this forum was not merely a scientific event. It was a turning point in terms of the internationalization of Azerbaijani science, the formation of national intellectual unity and the mobilization of diaspora strength through science.

The organizers of the forum - WAAS, the State Committee on Work with Diaspora and the Ministry of Science and Education - succeeded through this initiative in uniting Azerbaijani scientists working abroad around a single strategic agenda. With the participation of more than 150 Azerbaijani scientists and intellectuals from over 30 countries, the event became a unity of solidarity, science and national consciousness.

The opening ceremony took place at the Gulustan Palace in Baku. The message addressed by the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan, Mr. Ilham Aliyev, to the forum participants clearly showed the place of the event in state policy. The President drew special attention to the role that scientists would play in the restoration of Karabakh and emphasized the importance of involving intellectuals living abroad in the process of national development.

Within the forum, reports covering various scientific fields were presented. From artificial intelligence and data science to bioinformatics, green energy, climate change, diaspora policy, education systems and the protection of cultural heritage, the wide range of presentations once again demonstrated the level of integration of Azerbaijani scientists into the world's advanced knowledge systems.

At the plenary sessions, letters sent to the forum participants by Nobel laureates Aziz Sancar and Arieh Warshel confirmed the resonance the event created in international scientific circles.

One of the most impressive parts of the forum was the visits to Khankendi and Shusha on 11 September. In these cities, which have become symbols of free Karabakh, participants united their scientific and intellectual perspectives with love of the homeland. The visit to Karabakh University and the appeals voiced there revealed the desire of scientists abroad to participate in the reconstruction of these lands through their knowledge and experience.

The official formation of WAAS and the definition of its operating principles were among the most important achievements of the forum. The ideas expressed once again showed that Azerbaijan's scientific diaspora is no longer merely an observer, but an initiating and constructive partner. Azerbaijani science can strengthen its position not only domestically but also worldwide through the diaspora. Speaking in the language of science, this forum will play an important role in Azerbaijan's future global promotion and technological development.
