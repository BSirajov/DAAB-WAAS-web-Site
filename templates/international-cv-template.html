International CV template — usage guide This is a master reference covering sections commonly expected in academic, research, and senior professional CVs worldwide (EU, UK, US, UN agencies, and international organizations).
  • Delete sections you do not need; never omit content from your source document to fit the template.
  • Use reverse chronological order (most recent first) unless your field requires otherwise.
  • Date format: Month YYYY – Month YYYY (e.g., Jan 2020 – Present). Use en-dash (–), not hyphen (-).
  • Omit date of birth, marital status, photo, and full home address unless explicitly required in your target country.
  • Export to PDF via browser Print → Save as PDF for submission.

Professional Summary

Three to six sentences describing your professional identity, core expertise, career trajectory, major achievements, and the value you bring. Tailor this section to each application. For academic CVs, emphasize research program, teaching philosophy, and international collaboration. For industry roles, emphasize leadership, measurable outcomes, and domain expertise.

Publications

Use a consistent citation style (APA, Chicago, Vancouver, or field-specific) throughout. List all authors unless your field uses “et al.” after a threshold.
