Mission, Vision and Values

The values and goals guiding WAAS activities

Science, ethics and outlook

WAAS mission, vision and academic values are presented here on the basis of academic freedom, transparency, quality and responsibility, with a focus on advancing the international integration of Azerbaijani science.

WAAS institutional foundations

This page presents the Association's mission, vision and academic values within a single conceptual framework.

Mission Vision Values
🏛

Our mission

As a non-governmental organisation uniting Azerbaijani scholars living in countries around the world, the World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists supports Azerbaijan in developing and implementing strategic plans related to science and technology, energy, education, health, social sciences and culture.

🌐

Our vision

The World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists aspires to be recognised internationally for excellence in advancing scientific knowledge, promoting innovation, and nurturing scientists, engineers, artists and health professionals who can make a difference in Azerbaijan and worldwide. We want Azerbaijan to be known and accepted as a peace-loving state with a rich and deep history that has invested greatly in a prosperous and peaceful future for humanity. The world will benefit from Azerbaijan's participation on the global stage, and Azerbaijan will continue to draw on international experience to build a stronger state for future generations.

Explaining our values

These values are the core principles that shape WAAS's academic culture, model of cooperation and credibility in the international scientific community.

Academic freedom

  • Scientific research is conducted freely, without ideological, political or commercial pressure.
  • Scholars can express their scientific views without obstruction.

Academic ethics

  • Adherence to ethical standards in scientific work; avoidance of plagiarism and falsified results.
  • Objectivity in research and honest conduct of scholarly work.

Integrity

  • Scholars remain truthful in their research and academic discourse.
  • Errors are acknowledged and corrected on the basis of evidence.

Respect for diversity

  • Openness to different scientific approaches and alternative perspectives.
  • Tolerance and inclusion with regard to gender, ethnicity, religion, language, social status and other differences.

Quality

  • Research is carried out to high academic standards.
  • Use of methodologies and research principles accepted internationally.

Responsibility

  • Scholars take responsibility towards society, students and future generations.
  • Commitment to science that delivers practical and social benefit, not theory alone.

Excellence

  • Continuous learning, development and pursuit of higher scientific achievement.
  • Aiming for the best outcomes in research and teaching.

Dignity

  • Scholars conduct their professional work with high ethical standards.
  • Mutual respect and a supportive collective academic environment.

Transparency

  • Scientific work is conducted openly and clearly.
  • Objectivity and accountability in decision-making processes.
These values strengthen solidarity among scholars, build trust in the international scientific community
and establish sound academic traditions for young researchers.
