# Forum 2024 Strategic Implementation Framework

**Document type:** Institutional strategic implementation plan  
**Prepared for:** World Association of Azerbaijani Scientists (WAAS / DAAB)  
**Source basis:** First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad (September 2024, Baku) — strategic roadmap, plenary discussions, participant presentations, official addresses, and cooperation outcomes  
**Status:** Working framework for leadership review and partner engagement  
**Version:** 1.0 — May 2026

---

## Executive summary

The First Forum of Azerbaijani Scientists Living Abroad (Forum 2024) produced a consolidated **strategic roadmap** for the development of Azerbaijani science and education. Participant proposals were synthesised into nine thematic domains spanning education, research, digital infrastructure, diaspora integration, youth outreach, health and biotechnology, cultural heritage, governance, and regional development.

This document **transforms those roadmap themes into an implementable portfolio**: concrete projects, phased action plans, accountable stakeholder groups, WAAS coordination mechanisms, competency requirements, measurable outcomes, partnership channels, funding directions, and risk mitigation. It is intended not as a discussion summary, but as a **realistic execution framework** aligned with WAAS’s mission of mobilising diaspora scientific potential, strengthening institutional bridges, and advancing scientific diplomacy.

**Strategic intent:** Strengthen Azerbaijan’s integration into the global scientific arena; convert diaspora expertise into structured, measurable contributions to national priorities; and establish WAAS as a trusted coordinator between scientists abroad, universities, the State Committee on Diaspora Affairs, and the Ministry of Science and Education.

---

## 1. Purpose, scope, and alignment

### 1.1 Purpose

- Translate Forum 2024 roadmap goals into **operational projects** with owners, timelines, and indicators.
- Provide a single reference for **WAAS leadership**, **university rectors**, the **Diaspora Committee**, the **Ministry of Science and Education**, and **international partners**.
- Distinguish **conceptual directions** (vision-level) from **operational actions** (delivery-level).

### 1.2 Scope

| In scope | Out of scope (unless commissioned separately) |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Projects derivable from Forum 2024 roadmap and plenary themes | Detailed legal drafting of new legislation |
| WAAS-coordinated diaspora–institution programmes | Full capital investment business cases for energy infrastructure |
| Digital, education, research, and diplomacy initiatives | Replacement of state budget planning processes |

### 1.3 Alignment with WAAS mission and scientific diplomacy

Forum outcomes reinforce WAAS’s long-term role as:

1. **Network hub** — connecting 80+ countries’ Azerbaijani scientists with home institutions.
2. **Knowledge broker** — matching diaspora expertise to national research and education priorities.
3. **Scientific diplomacy channel** — presenting evidence-based narratives (including Karabakh reconstruction and cultural heritage) through credible academic voices abroad.
4. **Implementation partner** — not duplicating state agencies, but **coordinating** diaspora participation in programmes led by universities, ANAS, the Diaspora Committee, and ministries.

Official forum messaging (Presidential appeal, Nobel laureate addresses, plenary theme on *global academic networks*) consistently emphasises: **networking**, **institutional linkage**, **innovation**, and **youth**. This framework mirrors those priorities.

## 3. Governance and execution model

### 3.1 Recommended governance structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Strategic Steering Committee (SSC)                         │
│  WAAS Executive Board + invited: 2 rectors, Diaspora        │
│  Committee rep., Ministry liaison (observer)                │
└──────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────┘
                           │
┌──────────────────────────▼──────────────────────────────────┐
│  Implementation Secretariat (hosted by WAAS)                  │
│  Programme managers per domain; M&E officer; digital lead   │
└──────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────┘
                           │
        ┌──────────────────┼──────────────────┐
        ▼                  ▼                  ▼
   Domain Working      University          International
   Groups (DWGs)       Cluster Leads       Partner Desk
```

**Strategic Steering Committee (SSC)** — quarterly; approves portfolio, priorities, and major partnerships.  
**Implementation Secretariat** — day-to-day coordination, reporting, grant pipeline support.  
**Domain Working Groups (DWGs)** — thematic leads from WAAS membership; draft work plans; recruit experts.  
**University Cluster Leads** — rotating rector/academic council nominees per project cluster.  
**International Partner Desk** — ERASMUS/Horizon liaison, MOU tracking, embassy science attaché contact map.

### 3.2 Execution principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **WAAS coordinates, institutions deliver** | WAAS does not replace universities or ministries; it mobilises diaspora input and tracks commitments. |
| **Pilot → scale** | Every major initiative starts as a 12-month pilot with explicit go/no-go criteria. |
| **Open metrics** | Annual public implementation report (aligned with roadmap page updates). |
| **Ethics & integrity** | Research ethics, authorship standards, and anti-plagiarism norms embedded in publication support programmes. |

### 3.3 WAAS coordination mechanisms

| Mechanism | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Forum Implementation Registry** | Central register of projects, status, owners, KPIs (digital, maintained by Secretariat). |
| **Scientist–Institution Matchmaking Service** | Pairs diaspora experts to university/ANAS requests (building on existing WAAS directory). |
| **Quarterly Implementation Forum (virtual)** | Progress review; successor to ad-hoc post-Forum coordination. |
| **Rector Roundtable (annual)** | Aligns university clusters with national priorities; co-chaired with Ministry observer. |
| **Grant Support Unit** | Templates, partner finding, proposal clinics for Horizon Europe, ERASMUS+, bilateral funds. |
| **Working Groups on AI & Bioinformatics** | Cross-domain technical council (see Domain I). |

## 4. Thematic architecture

Nine forum roadmap domains are retained, with **three cross-cutting lenses**:

| Cross-cutting lens | Applies across |
|--------------------|----------------|
| **A. Artificial intelligence & data science** | Digital infrastructure, health/biotech, research, education |
| **B. Scientific diplomacy & narrative integrity** | International cooperation, culture, Karabakh/regional development |
| **C. Diaspora network capital** | All domains — mobility, mentoring, joint appointments |

| ID | Domain | Forum roadmap anchor | Priority tier* |
|----|--------|----------------------|----------------|
| I | Education & academic development | Joint degrees, exchanges, regional faculties | **P1** |

## 16. Risks, challenges, and mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Institutional fragmentation** | Duplication, slow MOUs | SSC + single Implementation Registry |
| **Volunteer burnout** | Delivery delays | Paid Secretariat core (even 2–3 FTE); rotate DWG chairs |
| **Low follow-through post-Forum** | Credibility loss | Quarterly virtual forum; public KPI report |
| **Funding gaps** | Pilot stall | Tiered portfolio; grant unit; in-kind university model |
| **Digital platform underuse** | Wasted investment | Co-design with scientists; integrate with grant & mentoring workflows |
| **Political sensitivity (diplomacy narratives)** | Reputational risk | Peer-reviewed briefs; academic tone; SSC clearance for external statements |
| **Quality concerns in publication support** | Ethics issues | Mentoring standards; plagiarism training; no ghost-writing |
| **Brain drain perception** | Domestic resistance | Emphasise “return & contribute” placements and joint appointments |
